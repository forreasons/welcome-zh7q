## Hard blockers (must fix before ship)

1. **QR code on the entry card still points to `https://thehassanfamily.com/`** — a placeholder domain that does not resolve. ENTRY_SETUP.md says swap before printing, but the SVG/PDF as delivered still embed the placeholder QR and the visible label reads `thehassanfamily.com`. Anyone scanning the printed card today gets a "page not found." Block printing until the real GitHub Pages URL (or custom domain) is encoded and the URL label is updated. This is the single most consequential item in the package.

2. **WiFi password is `[set me]`** on the live HTML (line 453). This is the first card a guest sees after the hero. If the site goes live with the placeholder, the WiFi card reads as broken on the very first tap. Replace before ship.

3. **Chapter 1 wedding month/year is `[the month and year]`** (line 496). Zunera knows this; needs the actual month and year (per the copy builder's brief, this was one of three approved bracketed prompts). Fill before ship.

4. **Art item 4 location is `[room]`** (line 1007). Same as above — approved placeholder, needs to be filled with the actual room the botanical print lives in.

5. **Bleed at trim on the entry card** — Builder B's flag is correct and is a real print-shop issue. The SVG is sized to 5x7 trim with no extra bleed margin. Moo, VistaPrint, and Mixam all require either 0.125" bleed on every side (so 5.25x7.25 actual artboard) or explicit "no bleed" trim with no design touching the edge. The keyline frame currently sits 90px (0.3") in from the edge, which is inside the safe area, but the cream background fills the full 5x7 — so when the printer trims, any drift will show as a thin white line on one or more edges. Either: (a) add 0.125" bleed by scaling artboard to 5.25x7.25 and extending the cream to the new edge, or (b) tell the print shop "no bleed, trim to file edge" and accept a slightly higher risk of edge artifacts. (a) is the standard fix.

## Soft flags (ship-okay but flag to Zunera)

1. **Chinoiserie line-art quality is uneven across the 8 illustrations.** Two of the eight self-flagged by the visual builder (Ch 2 dining-table reading "country French" rather than chinoiserie; Ch 3 Seattle firs reading generic) — I agree on both. The Ch 2 illustration is a folding table set for two with a hanging pendant lamp; it has zero chinoiserie register and reads as a Pinterest wedding clip-art table. The Ch 3 firs are stylized but generic Christmas-tree silhouettes rather than the Pacific Northwest mood the copy invokes. The other six (peony ornament, two-birds-on-branch, wreath monogram, coffee cup, Temecula hills, front door) land. Decision for Zunera: ship as-is (most guests will not register the inconsistency) or commission Hadiya to swap real photos into these slots once collected.

2. **Sharbat Rooh Afza print SVG shows a bottle silhouette with the word "Rooh Afza" in Latin script.** The real print at Zunera's home (per the style guide and the v0.1 review) is a vintage advertisement in Urdu. The art-meta copy says exactly that — "Framed vintage advertisement in Urdu" — but the SVG illustration is a bottle silhouette with English-letter "Rooh Afza" on the label. A guest scanning the page sees a bottle, not Urdu calligraphy, and the discrepancy registers if she compares the website to what's on the actual wall. This is the kind of detail Zunera would catch.

3. **Brass-on-cream contrast for small body text fails WCAG AA (4.5:1).** Brass `#A87E3F` on cream `#F5EFE3` measures roughly 3.5:1. The brass is used at 10-11px for small uppercase eyebrows ("OUR STORY," "ART IN OUR HOME," "ESTABLISHED 2024," section labels). At that size, AA requires 4.5:1. AA-large (3:1) only applies at 18pt+ or 14pt+ bold. This will not affect most guests but will be flagged by any accessibility tool and will be hard to read for guests with low vision. Mitigation: darken brass to `#8A6428` (about 5:1) for the small labels only, keep `#A87E3F` for keylines and dividers. Or accept it.

4. **No alt text on any of the SVGs.** Every illustration is marked `aria-hidden="true"`. That's defensible for purely decorative ornaments but borderline for the chapter illustrations and the art-gallery illustrations, which carry semantic content. A screen-reader user gets no signal that the wedding chapter has a "two birds on a flowering branch" illustration or that the art gallery has four pieces. Adding `<title>` elements inside the meaningful SVGs (chapter illustrations + art-gallery illustrations) and removing `aria-hidden` from those eight would close this.

5. **Mocktail / coffee swap by season** per STYLE_GUIDE — current site shows the mocktail as the signature plus two coffee options. Per the style guide, the seasonal signature swaps. Not a bug, just a reminder that this section needs a refresh when the season turns.

6. **"Established 2024" is on both the website hero and the entry card.** Per the Temecula chapter, "We moved into this house in May 2024." The wedding was earlier (Chapter One). "Established 2024" reads as if the marriage / home was established in 2024 — if Zunera and her husband married before 2024, this is technically misleading. Worth confirming whether "Established 2024" refers to "this house" or "the Hassans" — the framing is ambiguous.

7. **NFC sticker placement on the back of the card and the chinoiserie ginger jar medallion on the back face are competing.** The back is mostly center-weighted around the medallion (centered at y=1050 of 2100). The NFC sticker sits behind the card — but if guests are encouraged to tap a specific spot ("Tap your phone here" line on the front), the sticker placement under the medallion's center is correct. Worth noting in ENTRY_SETUP.md exactly where on the back to place the sticker (centered behind the QR slot on the front, which puts it ~y=1240, not behind the medallion center at y=1050).
